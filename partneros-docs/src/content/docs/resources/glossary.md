# Partner Program Glossary

## A

### ACV (Annual Contract Value)
The average annual revenue per customer contract. In partner programs, ACV helps determine partner tier requirements and commission structures.

**Why it matters:** Partners with higher ACV deals typically require more support and earn higher commissions.

**How to measure:** Total contract value ÷ contract term in years


### ACR (Annual Contract Renewal)
The yearly renewal value of an existing customer contract. Used to track partner-influenced retention.

**Why it matters:** High ACR indicates strong partner-customer relationships and product stickiness.

**How to measure:** Sum of all contracts up for renewal in a given year × renewal rate


### ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)
Predictable revenue expected to continue in the future, normalized to a one-year period. The primary metric for SaaS and subscription businesses.

**Why it matters:** Partner programs are often evaluated on their contribution to ARR growth.

**How to measure:** MRR × 12, or total recurring revenue normalized to annual


---

## B

### Bronze Tier
The entry-level tier in a partner program, typically requiring minimal commitment or certification.

**Why it matters:** Bronze tier provides a low-barrier entry for partners to start selling, while the company can qualify leads.

**How to measure:** Number of registered partners meeting minimum criteria (usually just signed agreement)


---

## C

### CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
Total cost of acquiring a new customer, including marketing, sales, and partner costs.

**Why it matters:** Partner programs are often justified by reducing CAC compared to direct sales.

**How to measure:** Total acquisition costs ÷ number of new customers acquired


### Channel Conflict
Tension or competition between direct sales teams and channel partners competing for the same customers or deals.

**Why it matters:** Unmanaged channel conflict destroys partner trust and kills program momentum.

**How to measure:** Number of deals disputed between direct and channel, deal registration override requests


### Churn Rate
The percentage of customers or partners who stop doing business with you over a given period.

**Why it matters:** High partner churn indicates program dissatisfaction or lack of value.

**How to measure:** (Partners lost ÷ total partners at start of period) × 100


### CLTV (Customer Lifetime Value)
The total revenue expected from a customer throughout their entire relationship with your company.

**Why it matters:** Helps justify partner investments by showing long-term value of partner-acquired customers.

**How to measure:** Average revenue per customer × average customer lifespan


### Commission
Financial compensation paid to partners for generating sales or revenue.

**Why it matters:** Commission structure directly impacts partner motivation and behavior.

**How to measure:** Revenue generated × commission rate


### Competitive Moat
A sustainable competitive advantage that protects your market position from competitors.

**Why it matters:** Partner programs can create moats through ecosystem lock-in.

**How to measure:** Qualitative assessment of barriers to competitor entry


---

## D

### Deal Registration
A process where partners register deals they're working on to receive protection from channel conflict and priority support.

**Why it matters:** Deal registration prevents channel conflict and incentivizes partner investment in opportunities.

**How to measure:** Number of deals registered ÷ total partner-sourced deals


### Direct Sales
Sales made by your internal sales team, as opposed to through partners.

**Why it matters:** Understanding direct vs. channel mix is critical for program ROI calculations.

**How to measure:** Revenue from direct sales team ÷ total revenue


---

## E

### Enablement
Resources, training, and tools provided to partners to help them sell more effectively.

**Why it matters:** Better-enabled partners close more deals and represent your brand well.

**How to measure:** Number of partners completing enablement ÷ total partners, deal size correlation


---

## G

### Gold Tier
The highest partner tier, typically requiring significant revenue commitment and certification.

**Why it matters:** Gold partners typically generate the majority of partner revenue.

**How to measure:** Number of partners meeting Gold criteria, revenue from Gold tier


---

## I

### ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
The description of the perfect customer for your product or service.

**Why it matters:** Partners should sell to customers who match your ICP for best outcomes.

**How to measure:** Qualitative, but track: partner deals matching ICP vs. total partner deals


### Influenced Revenue
See: Partner-Influenced Revenue


---

## L

### Lead
A potential customer who has shown interest in your product or service.

**Why it matters:** Leads are the foundation of partner pipeline.

**How to measure:** Number of leads generated, lead-to-opportunity conversion rate


### LTV (Lifetime Value)
See: CLTV


---

## M

### MDF (Marketing Development Funds)
Funds allocated by vendors to partners for co-marketing activities.

**Why it matters:** MDF incentivizes partner marketing investment and builds brand awareness.

**How to measure:** MDF allocated ÷ partner marketing activities completed


### MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)
Predictable revenue expected each month from subscriptions or contracts.

**Why it matters:** MRR shows the predictable, recurring nature of revenue—critical for business valuation.

**How to measure:** Sum of all recurring revenue normalized to monthly


---

## N

### NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)
A legal contract protecting confidential information shared between parties.

**Why it matters:** NDAs are typically the first step in partner onboarding to protect sensitive discussions.

**How to measure:** Number of NDAs executed ÷ partner prospects contacted


### Net Revenue
Total revenue minus returns, discounts, and allowances.

**Why it matters:** Net revenue shows true program value after accounting for churn and discounts.

**How to measure:** Gross revenue - returns - discounts - allowances


### NPS (Net Promoter Score)
A metric measuring customer or partner loyalty and satisfaction.

**Why it matters:** High partner NPS predicts partner retention and advocacy.

**How to measure:** % Promoters (9-10) - % Detractors (0-6)


---

## O

### Onboarding
The process of integrating new partners into your program and getting them ready to sell.

**Why it matters:** Faster onboarding means faster time-to-revenue.

**How to measure:** Days from agreement to first deal, or days to certification completion


---

## P

### Partner
A company that promotes or sells your products or services in exchange for compensation.

**Why it matters:** Partners extend your reach and capabilities without proportional cost increase.


### Partner-Influenced Revenue
Revenue from deals where a partner played a role in the sale, even if not the primary driver.

**Why it matters:** Influenced revenue captures the full impact of partner marketing and awareness.

**How to measure:** Deals with partner involvement (marketing, introductions, etc.) marked in CRM


### Partner Portal
A web-based platform where partners can access resources, register deals, track commissions, and manage their relationship.

**Why it matters:** Self-service portals reduce partner management overhead.

**How to measure:** Portal adoption rate, self-service transactions ÷ total transactions


### Partner Program
A structured system for recruiting, enabling, and compensating partners.

**Why it matters:** Programs provide consistency and scalability to channel efforts.


### Partner-Sourced Revenue
Revenue from deals where the partner was the primary driver or originator.

**Why it matters:** Sourced revenue shows direct partner contribution to new business.

**How to measure:** Deals marked as partner-sourced in CRM


### Partner Tier
A level within a partner program, typically based on commitment, revenue, or certification.

**Why it matters:** Tiers create progression incentives and differentiate support/compensation.

**How to measure:** Revenue by tier, number of partners by tier


### Pipeline
The total value of all deals in various stages of the sales process.

**Why it matters:** Pipeline shows future revenue potential from partners.

**How to measure:** Sum of all open partner opportunities × probability


### PRM (Partner Relationship Management)
Software system for managing partner relationships, recruitment, and performance.

**Why it matters:** PRM enables scale and automation of partner operations.

**How to measure:** PRM adoption rate, data completeness in PRM


### Prospect
A potential partner who has been identified but not yet recruited.

**Why it matters:** Prospecting is the first step in partner recruitment.

**How to measure:** Number of prospects identified, conversion rate to partner


---

## Q

### QBR (Quarterly Business Review)
A regular meeting between vendor and partner to review performance, set goals, and address issues.

**Why it matters:** QBRs maintain partner engagement and catch issues early.

**How to measure:** % of partners with QBRs completed each quarter


---

## R

### ROI (Return on Investment)
A measure of profitability calculated as (gain - cost) ÷ cost.

**Why it matters:** ROI justifies partner program investment to executives.

**How to measure:** (Program revenue - Program cost) ÷ Program cost


### Revenue Share
A percentage of revenue shared with partners for sales or referrals.

**Why it matters:** Revenue share aligns partner and vendor interests.

**How to measure:** Total revenue paid to partners ÷ total partner-generated revenue


---

## S

### SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)
The segment of TAM that you can realistically target with your products or services.

**Why it matters:** SAM is a more realistic market opportunity than TAM.

**How to measure:** TAM × % addressable by your go-to-market


### Sourced Revenue
See: Partner-Sourced Revenue


### SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)
The portion of SAM that you can realistically capture.

**Why it matters:** SOM sets realistic revenue targets.

**How to measure:** SAM × market share realistically achievable


### Silver Tier
The middle tier in a partner program, typically requiring moderate revenue and certification.

**Why it matters:** Silver partners often drive the most program revenue.

**How to measure:** Number of Silver partners, Silver tier revenue


### Sla (Service Level Agreement)
A contract defining expected service levels and remedies for failures.

**Why it matters:** SLAs define partner support expectations.

**How to measure:** SLA compliance rate, response time adherence


---

## T

### TAM (Total Addressable Market)
The total market demand for a product or service.

**Why it matters:** TAM establishes the ceiling for growth opportunities.

**How to measure:** Total market size × average price


### Territory
A geographic or vertical market assigned to a partner for exclusive or priority coverage.

**Why it matters:** Territories prevent conflict and encourage investment.

**How to measure:** Revenue by territory, partner investment by territory


### Time to Revenue
The time from partner signing to first deal closed.

**Why it matters:** Faster time to revenue improves partner satisfaction and ROI.

**How to measure:** First deal date - contract signed date


---

## V

### VAR (Value-Added Reseller)
A partner that adds value to products through customization, integration, or additional services.

**Why it matters:** VARs often serve complex enterprise deals.

**How to measure:** Number of VAR partners, revenue from VAR-sourced deals


---

## W

### Win Rate
The percentage of opportunities that result in closed-won deals.

**Why it matters:** Win rate shows partner selling effectiveness.

**How to measure:** (Deals won ÷ deals pursued) × 100


---

## Y

### YoY (Year over Year)
A comparison of a metric to the same period in the previous year.

**Why it matters:** YoY shows growth trajectory and momentum.

**How to measure:** (Current period - Prior year period) ÷ Prior year period × 100
