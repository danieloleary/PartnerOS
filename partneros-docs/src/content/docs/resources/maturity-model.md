# Appendix II: Conceptual Partner Program Maturity Model

This model provides a framework for understanding the evolution of a partner program and helps identify which areas of this blueprint to prioritize based on your current stage.

**Stage 1: Foundational (Ad-hoc & Reactive)**
* **Characteristics:**
  * Partnerships are often opportunistic, driven by individual relationships.
  * Limited formal processes or dedicated resources.
  * Basic partner agreements (NDA, referral agreement).
  * Success is inconsistently tracked, if at all.
  * Little to no specific partner enablement.
* **Blueprint Focus:**
  * Partner Business Case (I.1) - To justify investment.
  * Ideal Partner Profile (IPP) (I.2) - To define "good" early.
  * Partnership One-Pager (II.6) - For initial outreach.
  * Email Sequence (II.1) - For initial reachout.
* **Typical Duration:** 0-6 months

**Stage 2: Developing (Emerging Program)**
* **Characteristics:**
  * Formal partner program established.
  * Dedicated or partial-time resources for partners.
  * Basic enablement (product training).
  * Partner tiering (e.g., registered vs. certified).
  * Co-marketing begins.
  * Partner success metrics start being defined.
* **Blueprint Focus:**
  * Partner Strategy Plan (I.5) - Formalize vision.
  * Program Architecture (I.6) - Define tiers & benefits.
  * Partner Pitch Deck (II.5) - For sales teams.
  * Onboarding Checklist (II.9) - Standardize onboarding.
  * Training Deck (III.2) - Build enablement.
* **Typical Duration:** 6-18 months

**Stage 3: Defined (Scaling Program)**
* **Characteristics:**
  * Full-time partner team.
  * Multiple partner tiers with clear requirements.
  * Structured partner enablement & certification.
  * Partner QBRs established.
  * Partner-sourced revenue tracked.
  * Co-marketing campaigns.
  * Partner portal or PRM system.
* **Blueprint Focus:**
  * Certification Program (III.3) - Formalize certification.
  * Success Metrics (III.6) - Define KPIs.
  * QBR Template (III.7) - Structure QBRs.
  * Commission Structure (F.1) - Formalize compensation.
  * Deal Registration (O.1) - Protect partners.
* **Typical Duration:** 18-36 months

**Stage 4: Managed (Optimizing)**
* **Characteristics:**
  * Mature partner program with proven ROI.
  * Dedicated partner success team.
  * Advanced enablement (technical, sales).
  * Partner advisory board.
  * Joint GTM with top partners.
  * Multi-tier partner ecosystem (resellers, SI, ISV).
  * Partner API/integration program.
* **Blueprint Focus:**
  * Technical Integration Guide (III.5) - API partners.
  * Revenue Sharing (F.3) - Joint ventures.
  * Partner Health Scorecard (A.1) - Proactive health.
  * Board Deck (X.1) - Executive updates.
* **Typical Duration:** 36+ months

**Stage 5: Leading (Industry Standard)**
* **Characteristics:**
  * Recognized industry partner program.
  * Significant partner-sourced revenue (>30% of total).
  * Partner-driven product roadmap input.
  * Global partner network.
  * Annual partner summit.
  * Partner innovation lab.
* **Blueprint Focus:**
  * Use everything in the blueprint.
  * Consider custom templates for unique needs.
  * Build proprietary partner tools/APIs.
  * Create industry-specific playbooks.

---

*This model provides a general framework. Your specific progression may vary based on company size, resources, and strategic priorities.*
