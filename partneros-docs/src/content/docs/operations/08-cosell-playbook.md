Co-selling is where partner programs prove their value. This playbook turns "we have partners" into "we win with partners."

:::tip[Insider Tip]
The best co-sell relationships feel like one team. If there's friction, your process is broken. Simplify until it flows.
:::

## How to Use This Template

Use this playbook to train sales teams and partners on effective joint selling. Review quarterly.

### Why It Works

Companies with strong co-sell processes see 2-3x higher partner-influenced revenue. The process matters.

---

## Co-Sell Models

### Model 1: Partner-Led

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Who sells** | Partner leads the sale |
| **Your role** | Technical validation, pricing |
| **Best for** | Resellers, VARs |
| **Revenue split** | Partner 70%, You 30% |

### Model 2: Sales-Led

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Who sells** | Your team leads |
| **Your role** | Close, partner assists |
| **Best for** | Implementation partners |
| **Revenue split** | You 70%, Partner 30% |

### Model 3: True Co-Sell

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Who sells** | Both equally |
| **Your role** | Joint discovery |
| **Best for** | Strategic partners |
| **Revenue split** | 50/50 or by activity |

## The Co-Sell Process

### Stage 1: Opportunity Identification

| Step | Who | Action |
|------|-----|--------|
| Lead identified | Either | Register in CRM |
| Fit confirmed | Both | Validate ICP match |
| Strategy set | Both | Define roles |

**Timeline:** 1-2 days

---

### Stage 2: Discovery & Qual

| Step | Who | Action |
|------|-----|--------|
| Initial meeting | Both | Joint discovery call |
| Requirements doc | Partner | Document needs |
| Solution design | Both | Design the proposal |

**Timeline:** 3-5 days

---

### Stage 3: Proposal & Pricing

| Step | Who | Action |
|------|-----|--------|
| Proposal draft | Partner | Create first draft |
| Pricing approval | You | Validate deal economics |
| Legal review | Both | Contract review |

**Timeline:** 5-10 days

---

### Stage 4: Close

| Step | Who | Action |
|------|-----|--------|
| Presentation | Both | Joint demo |
| Negotiation | Both | Final terms |
| Close | You | Contract signature |

**Timeline:** 5-15 days

---

### Stage 5: Delivery

| Step | Who | Action |
|------|-----|--------|
| Implementation | Partner | Deploy solution |
| Support | Both | Ongoing success |
| QBR | Both | Review results |

## Role Definitions

### Your Sales Rep

- Owns customer relationship
- Drives timeline
- Approves pricing
- Signs contracts

### Partner Rep

- Provides local presence
- Delivers technical expertise
- Handles implementation
- Provides ongoing support

### Partner Manager

- Coordinates between teams
- Removes blockers
- Tracks pipeline
- Facilitates communication

## Common Challenges

### Challenge 1: Slow Response

**Problem:** Partner waits too long for pricing

**Solution:** Pre-approve pricing bands for partners

### Challenge 2: Unclear Roles

**Problem:** Both teams think the other is following up

**Solution:** Define RACI matrix for each deal

### Challenge 3: Deal Stalling

**Problem:** Opportunities die in the funnel

**Solution:** Weekly sync, clear escalation path

## What I Wish I Knew

The biggest mistake? Not defining clear roles. Ambiguity kills deals.

The second mistake? Over-complicating the process. If it's too hard, partners won't do it.

The third mistake? Not tracking partner attribution. You can't improve what you don't measure.
