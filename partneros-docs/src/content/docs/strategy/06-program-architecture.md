> **Your program architecture is a promise to partners. Make promises you can keep—and keep every promise you make. A broken promise destroys more trust than a bad product.** — Dan O'Leary

:::tip INSIDER TIP
The most common architecture failure? Three tiers that look identical. Bronze, Silver, and Gold must have meaningfully different benefits. If a partner can't articulate why they'd move from Silver to Gold, your tier structure is broken.
:::

## How to Use This Template

**Purpose:**
This template creates a structured partner program architecture that attracts, enables, and retains partners. It defines tiers, requirements, benefits, and progression paths.

**Why It Works:**
A clear architecture removes ambiguity. Partners know what they get at each tier, what it takes to advance, and what's expected. No guesswork, no politics—just clear standards.

**Steps:**

1. **Define tier structure** — How many tiers? What differentiates them? (1 hour)
2. **Set requirements** — What must partners achieve? (1 hour)
3. **Design benefits** — What do partners get? (2 hours)
4. **Create enablement path** — How do partners grow? (1 hour)
5. **Define metrics** — How do you measure tier success? (1 hour)

## Tier Structure

### Three-Tier Standard

Most programs use three tiers. More than three creates confusion; fewer doesn't create aspiration.

| Tier | Revenue Target | Focus | Time to Achieve |
|------|----------------|-------|-----------------|
| **Bronze** (Registered) | <$100K | Self-service, learning | Immediate |
| **Silver** (Certified) | $100K-$500K | Active selling, co-marketing | 12-18 months |
| **Gold** (Strategic) | $500K+ | Joint GTM, executive alignment | 24-36 months |

### Tier Definitions

**Bronze: Registered Partner**
Entry-level partners who are learning about your solutions. They're building capability with self-service resources.

- **Who:** New partners, small partners, geographic specialists
- **Focus:** Learning, certification, first deals
- **Commitment:** Low—register to access basic resources

**Silver: Certified Partner**
Partners actively selling and delivering your solutions. They've demonstrated commitment and capability.

- **Who:** Proven partners with certified professionals
- **Focus:** Growing revenue, co-marketing, customer success
- **Commitment:** Moderate—specific revenue targets, certification requirements

**Gold: Strategic Partner**
Elite partners with deep strategic alignment. Joint go-to-market, executive sponsorship, custom enablement.

- **Who:** Top-performing partners with strategic fit
- **Focus:** Joint GTM, market expansion, innovation
- **Commitment:** High—executive sponsorship, dedicated resources

## Tier Requirements

### Requirements by Tier

Each tier has specific requirements partners must meet:

| Requirement | Bronze | Silver | Gold |
|-------------|--------|--------|------|
| **Revenue** | None | $100K/year | $500K/year |
| **Certification** | 1 certified | 3 certified | 5+ certified |
| **Customers** | 1 reference | 5 references | 10+ references |
| **QBR** | Annual | Semi-annual | Quarterly |
| **Executive** | None | VP-level | C-level |

### Revenue Tiers

Revenue thresholds create objective progression:

```markdown
## Silver Tier Requirements

### Revenue
- Minimum $100K annual partner-sourced revenue
- Must maintain for 2 consecutive quarters to qualify
- Calculated on rolling 12-month basis

### Certification  
- Minimum 3 certified professionals
- At least 1 Advanced certification
- Annual recertification required
```

### Non-Revenue Requirements

Revenue isn't everything. Include capability requirements:

| Requirement | Bronze | Silver | Gold |
|-------------|--------|--------|------|
| Customer references | 0 | 2 | 5 |
| QBR participation | Optional | Required | Required |
| Marketing participation | Optional | 2 campaigns/year | Quarterly |
| Deal registration | Standard | Priority | Exclusivity |

## Benefits Framework

### Benefits by Tier

Benefits must justify the requirements—and increase as partners invest more:

| Benefit | Bronze | Silver | Gold |
|---------|--------|--------|------|
| **Deal registration** | Standard queue | Priority (30 days) | Exclusivity (60 days) |
| **Discount** | 10% | 15% | 20%+ |
| **Co-marketing funds** | $0 | $10K/year | $50K+/year |
| **Partner manager** | No dedicated | Shared | Dedicated |
| **Training** | Self-service | Instructor-led | Custom |
| **Support** | Business hours | Extended | 24/7 |

### Sales Benefits

**Deal registration** is the core sales benefit:

| Feature | Bronze | Silver | Gold |
|---------|--------|--------|------|
| Protection window | None | 30 days | 60 days |
| Discount protection | Standard | Preferred | Exclusive |
| Competitor override | Standard rules | Hard to override | Nearly impossible |

### Financial Benefits

| Benefit | Bronze | Silver | Gold |
|---------|--------|--------|------|
| Base discount | 10% | 15% | 20% |
| Rebate | None | 5% > $200K | 10% > $500K |
| Marketing funds | Access only | $10K/year | $50K+/year |
| MDF access | No | Yes | Yes + matching |

## Growth Path

### Tier Progression

Clear progression creates aspiration:

```markdown
## Bronze → Silver Progression

### Requirements to Advance
1. Achieve $100K revenue in 12 months
2. Maintain revenue for 2 consecutive quarters  
3. 3 certified professionals (1 Advanced minimum)
4. Participate in 2 co-marketing activities
5. Complete QBR with partner manager

### Timeline
- Typical progression: 12-18 months
- Fast-track: 9 months with exceptional performance
- Extended: Up to 24 months with documented challenges
```

### Progression Metrics

Track what predicts tier success:

| Metric | Predicts Silver | Predicts Gold |
|--------|----------------|---------------|
| Time to first deal | <60 days | <45 days |
| Certification velocity | 3 certs in 6 months | 5 certs in 9 months |
| Deal registration usage | >50% deals | >70% deals |
| QBR completion | On-time | On-time + prep |

### Demotion and Exit

Define what happens when partners fall behind:

```markdown
## Silver → Bronze Demotion

### Triggers
- Revenue below $75K for 2 consecutive quarters
- Certification lapse (below requirements)
- No QBR participation for 2 cycles

### Process
1. 30-day notice with improvement plan
2. 90-day remediation period
3. Demotion if metrics not met
4. 6-month path to re-earn Silver
```

## What I Wish I Knew

**Tiers must have meaningful difference:**
Early in my career, I created Bronze/Silver/Gold where the only difference was 5% discount. Partners saw through it immediately. Now, each tier has at least 3 meaningfully different benefits.

**Requirements without enforcement are suggestions:**
If you say partners need 3 certifications to be Silver but don't audit it, no one gets certified. Build enforcement into your CRM and deal registration system.

**Demotion is harder than promotion:**
Have clear demotion criteria before you launch. It's easier to prevent tier creep than to push partners down later.

## Common Pitfalls

**Pitfall #1: Tiers Without Aspiration**
- **What happens:** Partners plateau at Silver, no one wants Gold
- **Warning sign:** 80% of partners at Silver, <5% at Gold
- **Prevention:** Make Gold meaningfully different

**Pitfall #2: Benefits Without Requirements**
- **What happens:** Partners get benefits without delivering value
- **Warning sign:** Revenue requirements not enforced
- **Prevention:** Link every benefit to a requirement

**Pitfall #3: Static Architecture**
- **What happens:** Architecture doesn't evolve with market
- **Warning sign:** Same tier structure for 3+ years
- **Prevention:** Annual architecture review
