## How to Use This Template

**Purpose:**
Use this template to define, track, and communicate key performance indicators for partner success, enabling data-driven decisions and effective partner management.

**Steps:**
1. Select the metrics most relevant to your partner program goals.
2. Establish baselines and targets for each metric.
3. Set up tracking mechanisms in your PRM/CRM.
4. Create dashboards for internal teams and partner visibility.
5. Review metrics regularly and adjust strategies based on insights.

# Partner Success Metrics Dashboard

## 1. Metrics Framework Overview

### Why Metrics Matter
Effective partner programs are built on clear, measurable outcomes. A robust metrics framework enables you to:

- **Identify** top-performing partners for investment
- **Diagnose** underperforming partnerships early
- **Demonstrate** ROI to leadership
- **Align** partner incentives with business goals
- **Optimize** program resources and strategies

### Metrics Categories
| Category | Focus Area | Key Question |
|----------|------------|--------------|
| Revenue | Financial contribution | How much business are partners driving? |
| Pipeline | Future opportunity | What's the partner-influenced sales funnel? |
| Engagement | Program participation | Are partners actively involved? |
| Enablement | Capability building | Are partners equipped to succeed? |
| Satisfaction | Relationship health | How do partners feel about the program? |
| Operational | Efficiency | How well are we executing? |

## 2. Revenue Metrics

### Primary Revenue KPIs

| Metric | Definition | Formula | Target Range |
|--------|------------|---------|--------------|
| **Partner-Sourced Revenue** | Revenue from deals originated by partners | Sum of closed-won where partner = source | 20-40% of total revenue |
| **Partner-Influenced Revenue** | Revenue where partner assisted but didn't source | Sum of closed-won where partner involved | 30-50% of total revenue |
| **Partner Revenue Growth** | YoY growth in partner-driven revenue | (Current year - Prior year) / Prior year | 25%+ annually |
| **Revenue per Partner** | Average revenue per active partner | Total partner revenue / Active partners | $X per partner |
| **Partner Attach Rate** | % of deals with partner involvement | Deals with partner / Total deals | 40-60% |

## 3. Pipeline Metrics

### Pipeline KPIs

| Metric | Definition | Formula | Target |
|--------|------------|---------|--------|
| **Partner-Sourced Pipeline** | Total value of open opportunities sourced by partners | Sum of open opps where partner = source | 3x revenue target |
| **Pipeline Coverage Ratio** | Pipeline to quota coverage | Partner pipeline / Partner revenue target | 3-4x |
| **Deal Registration Volume** | Number of deals registered | Count of registrations per period | Growth MoM |
| **Registration-to-Close Rate** | % of registered deals that close | Closed-won / Total registered | 25-35% |
| **Average Deal Size** | Mean value of partner deals | Total revenue / Number of deals | ≥ Direct deals |
| **Sales Cycle Length** | Time from registration to close | Avg days from registration to close | ≤ Direct deals |

## 4. Engagement Metrics

### Partner Engagement KPIs

| Metric | Definition | Formula | Target |
|--------|------------|---------|--------|
| **Active Partner Rate** | % of partners with activity in period | Partners with activity / Total partners | 70%+ |
| **Portal Login Frequency** | Average logins per partner per month | Total logins / Active partners | 4+ per month |
| **Content Engagement** | Downloads, views of partner resources | Total engagements per period | Growth MoM |
| **Event Participation** | Attendance at partner events/webinars | Attendees / Invited partners | 40%+ |
| **Deal Registration Activity** | Partners submitting deal registrations | Partners with registrations / Total | 50%+ |

### Engagement Scoring Model
```
Partner Engagement Score (0-100)

Component                    Weight    Scoring
─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Marketing Participation      15%       Co-marketing activities
Communication Response       10%       Email/call response rate
Event Attendance            10%       Events attended/available

Score Interpretation:
- 80-100: Highly Engaged (invest more)
- 60-79: Engaged (maintain)
- 40-59: At Risk (intervene)
- 0-39: Disengaged (re-evaluate)
```

## 6. Satisfaction Metrics

### Partner Satisfaction KPIs

| Metric | Definition | Formula | Target |
|--------|------------|---------|--------|
| **Partner NPS** | Net Promoter Score from partner surveys | % Promoters - % Detractors | 40+ |
| **Partner CSAT** | Satisfaction rating (1-5 scale) | Average rating | 4.2+ |
| **Support Satisfaction** | Rating of support experience | Avg support survey rating | 4.5+ |
| **Program Satisfaction** | Overall program rating | Annual survey rating | 4.0+ |
| **Renewal/Retention Rate** | % of partners renewing agreements | Renewed / Eligible for renewal | 85%+ |

### NPS Survey Framework
```
Primary Question:
"On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend our partner
program to another company?"

Follow-up Questions:
1. What is the primary reason for your score?
2. What could we do to improve your experience?
3. What do you value most about our partnership?

Score Breakdown:
- Promoters (9-10): Enthusiastic partners, likely to refer
- Passives (7-8): Satisfied but not enthusiastic
- Detractors (0-6): Unhappy, risk of churn

Survey Cadence: Quarterly pulse + Annual comprehensive
```

## 10. Using Metrics for Action

### Metrics-Driven Interventions

| Signal | Possible Causes | Recommended Actions |
|--------|----------------|---------------------|
| Declining revenue | Market shift, competitive pressure, disengagement | QBR, joint planning, competitive analysis |
| Low engagement | Poor onboarding, unclear value, resource constraints | Enablement review, success planning |
| Certification gaps | Training barriers, priority misalignment | Simplified paths, incentives |
| Low satisfaction | Unmet expectations, support issues, communication gaps | Executive outreach, program feedback session |
| Long sales cycles | Skill gaps, qualification issues, process friction | Sales enablement, deal support |

### Quarterly Review Process
```
1. PREPARE (Week before)
   - Pull all partner metrics
   - Identify trends and anomalies
   - Draft talking points and recommendations

2. REVIEW (Internal)
   - Team review of partner portfolio
   - Segment partners by health/performance
   - Prioritize intervention targets

3. PARTNER QBR
   - Share performance dashboard
   - Discuss achievements and challenges
   - Align on next quarter priorities
   - Document commitments

4. FOLLOW-UP (Week after)
   - Send QBR summary
   - Create action items
   - Schedule check-ins
   - Update partner plan
```
