    How to Use This Template¶

    Purpose: Use this template to define, track, and communicate key performance indicators for partner success, enabling data-driven decisions and effective partner management.

    Steps: 1. Select the metrics most relevant to your partner program goals. 2. Establish baselines and targets for each metric. 3. Set up tracking mechanisms in your PRM/CRM. 4. Create dashboards for internal teams and partner visibility. 5. Review metrics regularly and adjust strategies based on insights.

    Partner Success Metrics Dashboard¶

    1. Metrics Framework Overview¶

    Why Metrics Matter¶

    Effective partner programs are built on clear, measurable outcomes. A robust metrics framework enables you to:

    • Identify top-performing partners for investment
    • Diagnose underperforming partnerships early
    • Demonstrate ROI to leadership
    • Align partner incentives with business goals
    • Optimize program resources and strategies

    Metrics Categories¶

    Category Focus Area Key Question
    Revenue Financial contribution How much business are partners driving?
    Pipeline Future opportunity What's the partner-influenced sales funnel?
    Engagement Program participation Are partners actively involved?
    Enablement Capability building Are partners equipped to succeed?
    Satisfaction Relationship health How do partners feel about the program?
    Operational Efficiency How well are we executing?

    NPS Survey Framework¶

    Primary Question:
"On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend our partner
program to another company?"

Follow-up Questions:
1. What is the primary reason for your score?
2. What could we do to improve your experience?
3. What do you value most about our partnership?

Score Breakdown:
- Promoters (9-10): Enthusiastic partners, likely to refer
- Passives (7-8): Satisfied but not enthusiastic
- Detractors (0-6): Unhappy, risk of churn

Survey Cadence: Quarterly pulse + Annual comprehensive

    10. Using Metrics for Action¶

    Metrics-Driven Interventions¶

    Signal Possible Causes Recommended Actions
    Declining revenue Market shift, competitive pressure, disengagement QBR, joint planning, competitive analysis
    Low engagement Poor onboarding, unclear value, resource constraints Enablement review, success planning
    Certification gaps Training barriers, priority misalignment Simplified paths, incentives
    Low satisfaction Unmet expectations, support issues, communication gaps Executive outreach, program feedback session
    Long sales cycles Skill gaps, qualification issues, process friction Sales enablement, deal support

    Quarterly Review Process¶

    1. PREPARE (Week before)
   - Pull all partner metrics
   - Identify trends and anomalies
   - Draft talking points and recommendations

2. REVIEW (Internal)
   - Team review of partner portfolio
   - Segment partners by health/performance
   - Prioritize intervention targets

3. PARTNER QBR
   - Share performance dashboard
   - Discuss achievements and challenges
   - Align on next quarter priorities
   - Document commitments

4. FOLLOW-UP (Week after)
   - Send QBR summary
   - Create action items
   - Schedule check-ins
   - Update partner plan
