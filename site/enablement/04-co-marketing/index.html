    How to Use This Template¶

    Purpose: Use this playbook to establish guidelines, processes, and best practices for joint marketing activities between your company and partners.

    Steps: 1. Define your co-marketing program structure and available activities. 2. Establish brand guidelines and approval processes. 3. Set up MDF (Market Development Funds) request and reimbursement workflows. 4. Create campaign templates and asset libraries for partner use. 5. Implement tracking and ROI measurement for all co-marketing activities.

    Co-Marketing Playbook¶

    1. Co-Marketing Program Overview¶

    Purpose & Value¶

    Co-marketing amplifies reach, shares costs, and builds credibility by combining your brand strength with your partners' market presence and customer relationships.

    Benefits for Your Company: - Extended market reach through partner channels - Shared marketing investment and reduced cost per lead - Local market expertise and presence - Increased brand awareness in partner ecosystems

    Benefits for Partners: - Access to your marketing resources and expertise - Co-branded content and professional assets - Financial support through MDF programs - Demand generation for their pipeline

    Program Principles¶

    • Brand Integrity: All co-marketing must adhere to brand guidelines
    • Mutual Benefit: Activities should generate value for both parties
    • Measurable Impact: Track and report on all co-marketing ROI
    • Scalable Process: Standardized workflows that work for all partner tiers

    2. Co-Marketing Activities by Type¶

    Tier 1: Self-Service (All Partners)¶

    Partners can execute independently with provided assets.

    Activity Description Support Provided
    Social Media Share pre-approved posts and content Social toolkit, graphics, copy
    Blog Syndication Republish approved blog content Blog templates, syndication rights
    Email Templates Send co-branded emails to their lists Email templates, subject lines
    Case Studies Share customer success stories Co-branded case study PDFs
    Product Sheets Distribute solution briefs Co-branded collateral

    Tier 2: Supported (Silver+ Partners)¶

    Joint activities with your marketing team involvement.

    Activity Description Support Provided
    Webinars Joint educational webinars Promotion, platform, content support
    Local Events Sponsorship of partner-led events Collateral, speaker support, MDF
    Content Collaboration Co-authored blogs or whitepapers Writing support, design, promotion
    Digital Campaigns Joint paid media campaigns Campaign management, creative

    Tier 3: Strategic (Gold+ Partners)¶

    High-investment, high-visibility joint initiatives.

    Activity Description Support Provided
    Joint Solution Launch Marketing for new joint offerings Full campaign, PR, analyst relations
    Executive Events C-level roundtables or dinners Event management, executive participation
    Industry Conferences Joint booth or speaking opportunities Booth space, collateral, staffing
    Integrated Campaigns Multi-channel demand gen programs Full campaign orchestration

    3. Brand Guidelines for Partners¶

    Logo Usage¶

    DO:
- Use approved co-branded logo lockups
- Maintain minimum clear space around logos
- Use logos on approved background colors
- Scale logos proportionally

DON'T:
- Alter logo colors or proportions
- Place logos on busy backgrounds
- Use outdated logo versions
- Create unauthorized logo combinations

    Co-Branding Standards¶

    Element Guideline
    Logo Placement Your logo left, partner logo right (or as specified)
    Logo Size Equal visual weight for both logos
    Color Usage Primary brand colors only, no alterations
    Typography Approved fonts only (provide font files)
    Imagery Use approved image library or partner-provided

    Messaging Guidelines¶

    • Always use approved product names and descriptions
    • Include required legal disclaimers and trademarks
    • Avoid comparative claims without approval
    • Submit all external-facing content for review

    Asset Library Access¶

    Provide partners access to: - [ ] Logo files (various formats: PNG, SVG, EPS) - [ ] Brand guidelines document - [ ] Approved photography and illustrations - [ ] PowerPoint/Google Slides templates - [ ] Email templates - [ ] Social media graphics and copy - [ ] Product screenshots and demo videos

    4. Market Development Funds (MDF)¶

    MDF Program Structure¶

    Partner Tier Annual MDF Allocation Funding Model
    Registered Not eligible -
    Silver Up to $5,000 50% co-investment
    Gold Up to $15,000 50% co-investment
    Platinum Up to $50,000+ 50-75% co-investment

    Eligible Activities¶

    Approved for MDF: - Joint webinars and virtual events - Local events and trade shows - Digital advertising (PPC, social, display) - Content development (case studies, whitepapers) - Direct mail campaigns - Partner website development (solution pages)

    Not Eligible for MDF: - General partner overhead or salaries - Non-marketing travel expenses - Activities not featuring your joint value proposition - Alcohol or entertainment expenses - Activities without lead capture mechanism

    MDF Request Process¶

    Step 1: Submit Request (30+ days before activity)
        Partner completes MDF request form with:
        - Activity description and objectives
        - Target audience and expected reach
        - Detailed budget breakdown
        - Lead capture and follow-up plan
        - Metrics for success measurement

Step 2: Review & Approval (5-7 business days)
        Partner marketing team reviews for:
        - Strategic alignment
        - Budget appropriateness
        - Brand compliance
        - Expected ROI

Step 3: Execution
        Partner executes activity per approved plan
        Your team provides agreed support

Step 4: Proof of Performance (30 days post-activity)
        Partner submits:
        - Receipts and invoices
        - Screenshots/photos of activity
        - Lead list with required fields
        - Results summary

Step 5: Reimbursement (30 days after POP approval)
        Finance processes reimbursement to partner

    MDF Request Form Fields¶

    Partner Information:
- Partner company name
- Contact name and email
- Partner tier

Activity Details:
- Activity type (from approved list)
- Activity name/description
- Target audience and geography
- Proposed date(s)
- Expected attendees/reach

Budget:
- Total activity cost
- MDF amount requested
- Partner co-investment amount
- Line-item budget breakdown

Goals & Measurement:
- Number of leads expected
- Other KPIs (registrations, attendees, etc.)
- Follow-up plan for leads generated

Attachments:
- Draft creative/messaging (for review)
- Vendor quotes (if applicable)

    5. Campaign Playbooks¶

    Playbook 1: Joint Webinar¶

    Timeline: 6-8 weeks

    Week Your Team Partner Team
    1-2 Define topic, secure speakers Identify target audience, co-speaker
    3-4 Create content, landing page Promote to their database
    5-6 Set up platform, rehearsal Continue promotion
    7 Host webinar Co-present content
    8 Send recording, leads to partner Follow up on leads

    Success Metrics: - Registrations: 200+ - Attendance rate: 40%+ - Leads generated: 80+ - Pipeline influenced: $X

    Playbook 2: Local Event¶

    Timeline: 8-12 weeks

    Phase Activities
    Planning Select venue, define agenda, set budget
    Promotion Email campaigns, social, partner outreach
    Execution Event logistics, presentations, demos
    Follow-up Lead distribution, nurture campaigns

    Success Metrics: - Attendees: 50+ (varies by event type) - Lead quality: 60%+ fit ICP - Follow-up meetings booked: 10+

    Playbook 3: Joint Content Campaign¶

    Timeline: 4-6 weeks

    Week Activities
    1 Define topic, outline, assign sections
    2-3 Draft content, internal reviews
    4 Design, final approvals
    5-6 Publish, promote across channels

    Content Types: - Co-authored blog posts - Joint whitepapers or eBooks - Customer case studies - Solution briefs - Infographics

    6. Lead Management & Attribution¶

    Lead Capture Requirements¶

    All co-marketing activities must capture: - First name, last name - Company name - Job title - Business email - Phone number (optional but preferred) - Consent for marketing communications

    Lead Routing¶

    Lead Source           Routing
-----------------------------------------
Your-hosted webinar   Your CRM, share with partner
Partner-hosted event  Partner receives first, copy to your CRM
Joint campaign        Split based on agreed rules

    Attribution Model¶

    Scenario Attribution
    Partner sources lead, you close Partner-sourced
    Joint activity generates lead Partner-influenced
    Your campaign, partner assists close Partner-assisted

    SLA for Lead Follow-up¶

    • Hot leads: Contact within 24 hours
    • Warm leads: Contact within 48 hours
    • Nurture leads: Add to appropriate nurture track

    7. Approval Workflows¶

    Content Approval Process¶

    Content Type Approval Required Turnaround
    Social posts (templated) None (use as-is) Immediate
    Email (templated) None (use as-is) Immediate
    Custom content Partner Marketing Manager 5 business days
    Press releases PR + Legal 10 business days
    Customer quotes/logos Customer Success + Legal 5 business days

    Escalation Path¶

    1. Partner Marketing Manager (first review)
    2. Partner Marketing Director (budget > $10K or strategic)
    3. VP Marketing (press releases, major campaigns)

    8. Measuring Co-Marketing Success¶

    KPIs to Track¶

    Metric Definition Target
    Leads Generated Total new contacts from activity Activity-specific
    Cost Per Lead Total spend / leads generated < $100
    Pipeline Created $ value of opportunities 5x MDF investment
    Win Rate % of leads that close Benchmark to direct
    Partner Engagement % of partners using co-marketing 40%+ of Gold/Platinum

    Reporting Cadence¶

    • Monthly: Activity summary, leads generated, MDF utilization
    • Quarterly: ROI analysis, top-performing partners, program improvements
    • Annually: Full program review, budget planning, strategy refinement

    ROI Calculation¶

    Co-Marketing ROI = (Pipeline Created - Total Investment) / Total Investment x 100

Example:
- MDF Spent: $10,000
- Your Additional Investment: $5,000
- Pipeline Created: $150,000
- ROI = ($150,000 - $15,000) / $15,000 x 100 = 900%
