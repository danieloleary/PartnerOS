    How to Use This Template¶

    Purpose: Use this template to conduct structured quarterly business reviews with your partners. QBRs are critical for maintaining alignment, celebrating wins, addressing challenges, and planning future initiatives.

    Steps: 1. Gather performance data 2 weeks before the QBR 2. Pre-populate sections with metrics and talking points 3. Share agenda with partner 1 week in advance 4. Conduct the QBR collaboratively 5. Document action items and follow up within 48 hours 6. Reference the "Related Templates" for supporting information

    Appendix A: QBR Preparation Checklist¶

    2 Weeks Before¶

    • Pull revenue and pipeline data from CRM
    • Gather certification and training status
    • Collect marketing activity results
    • Review open support tickets and escalations
    • Compile win/loss analysis
    • Review last QBR action items

    1 Week Before¶

    • Complete draft QBR document
    • Send agenda and pre-read to partner
    • Confirm attendees and logistics
    • Prepare presentation materials
    • Brief internal stakeholders

    Day of QBR¶

    • Test video/audio if virtual
    • Have backup contact info
    • Bring printed materials (if in-person)
    • Designate note-taker
    • Set up action item tracking

    Within 48 Hours After¶

    • Send thank you and summary email
    • Distribute meeting notes and action items
    • Update CRM with relevant information
    • Schedule follow-up meetings as needed
    • Enter next QBR on calendar

    Appendix B: QBR Best Practices¶

    Do's¶

    • Be Data-Driven: Come with facts, not opinions
    • Celebrate Wins: Start positive, acknowledge successes
    • Be Transparent: Share challenges openly, build trust
    • Listen Actively: QBRs should be 50/50 dialogue
    • Focus on Solutions: For every problem, propose options
    • Document Everything: Detailed notes enable accountability
    • Follow Through: Action items without follow-up erode trust

    Don'ts¶

    • Don't Surprise: Share difficult news before the meeting
    • Don't Lecture: Avoid one-way presentations
    • Don't Skip Prep: Unprepared QBRs waste everyone's time
    • Don't Ignore Elephants: Address tension or issues directly
    • Don't Over-Promise: Set realistic expectations
    • Don't Forget the Relationship: Business aside, maintain connection

    Common QBR Pitfalls to Avoid¶

    Pitfall Impact Prevention
    Data inconsistency Lost credibility Validate data sources beforehand
    Too many attendees Unfocused discussion Limit to key stakeholders
    No executive presence Lack of commitment Require exec sponsors
    Skipping action review Erodes accountability Always start with last QBR items
    Running over time Frustrated attendees Strict timekeeping, parking lot
