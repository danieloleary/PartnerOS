    How to Use This Template¶

    Purpose: Use this Data Processing Agreement to ensure compliance with GDPR and other privacy regulations when processing personal data.

    Steps: 1. Identify data processing activities 2. Customize for specific data types 3. Have legal/privacy team review 4. Execute with partner

    Data Processing Agreement¶

    PARTIES¶

    This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") is entered into as of [Date] and forms part of the Master Service Agreement dated [MSA Date] between:

    [Your Company Name] ("Data Controller" or "Company") AND [Partner Company Name] ("Data Processor" or "Partner")

    1. DEFINITIONS¶

    "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person as defined under applicable data protection laws (including GDPR).

    "Processing" means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, storage, modification, disclosure, deletion, or transfer.

    "Data Protection Laws" means GDPR, CCPA, and other applicable privacy regulations.

    "Sub-processor" means any third party engaged by Partner to process Personal Data.

    2. SCOPE AND PURPOSE¶

    2.1 Processing Activities¶

    Partner shall process Personal Data only for the following purposes: - [Delivery of contracted services] - [Customer support] - [Account management] - [Marketing (with consent)]

    2.2 Data Categories¶

    Category Examples
    Contact Data Name, email, phone, address
    Account Data Company name, job title
    Usage Data Login timestamps, feature usage
    Transaction Data Order history, payment info

    2.3 Data Subjects¶

    • Customers
    • Leads
    • Employees of End Customers
    • Other: [Specify]

    3. OBLIGATIONS OF DATA PROCESSOR¶

    Partner shall:

    3.1 Processing¶

    • Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Company
    • Ensure personnel are subject to confidentiality obligations
    • Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures

    3.2 Security Measures¶

    Measure Implementation
    Encryption TLS 1.2+ for data in transit; AES-256 for data at rest
    Access Control Role-based access, MFA required
    Logging Audit trails for all data access
    Incident Response 24-hour notification of breaches

    3.3 Sub-Processors¶

    • Partner may engage Sub-processors with prior written consent
    • Partner remains fully liable for Sub-processor compliance
    • Current Sub-processors: [List or attach]

    3.4 Data Subject Rights¶

    Partner shall assist Company in responding to data subject requests within [30] days: - Access requests - Rectification requests - Erasure requests - Data portability requests - Objection to processing

    4. DATA TRANSFERS¶

    4.1 Transfers Outside EEA¶

    [ ] Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) apply [ ] Adequacy decision in place [ ] Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs) apply

    4.2 Transfer Mechanism¶

    For transfers outside the EEA, the following safeguards apply: [Describe]

    5. BREACH NOTIFICATION¶

    5.1 Notification Timeline¶

    Partner shall notify Company within [24-72] hours of becoming aware of any Personal Data breach.

    5.2 Notification Contents¶

    • Nature of the breach
    • Categories and approximate number of data subjects affected
    • Likely consequences
    • Measures taken to address the breach

    6. AUDITS AND INSPECTIONS¶

    6.1 Audit Rights¶

    Company has the right to: - Conduct audits [annually / upon reasonable request] - Request third-party audit reports (SOC 2, ISO 27001) - Review security policies and practices

    6.2 Certification¶

    Partner maintains: - [ ] SOC 2 Type II - [ ] ISO 27001 - [ ] Other: [Specify]

    7. TERM AND TERMINATION¶

    7.1 Term¶

    This DPA shall remain in effect for the duration of the underlying MSA.

    7.2 Termination¶

    Upon termination, Partner shall: - Delete or return all Personal Data within [30] days - Provide written certification of deletion - Continue to protect Personal Data as required by law

    8. LIABILITY¶

    Liability under this DPA shall be subject to the limitations set forth in the MSA.

    9. GOVERNING LAW¶

    This DPA shall be governed by the laws of [State/Country].

    PARTNER:

    Signature: _______________________ Name: _______________________ Title: _______________________ Date: _______________________

    Appendix: Sub-Processor List¶

    Checklist¶

    • Identify all data processing activities
    • Define data categories and subjects
    • Set appropriate security measures
    • Establish breach notification procedures
    • Define audit rights
    • Have legal/privacy review
    • Execute and store with MSA
