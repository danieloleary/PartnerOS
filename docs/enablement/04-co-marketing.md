## How to Use This Template

**Purpose:**
Use this playbook to establish guidelines, processes, and best practices for joint marketing activities between your company and partners.

**Steps:**
1. Define your co-marketing program structure and available activities.
2. Establish brand guidelines and approval processes.
3. Set up MDF (Market Development Funds) request and reimbursement workflows.
4. Create campaign templates and asset libraries for partner use.
5. Implement tracking and ROI measurement for all co-marketing activities.

---

# Co-Marketing Playbook

## 1. Co-Marketing Program Overview

### Purpose & Value
Co-marketing amplifies reach, shares costs, and builds credibility by combining your brand strength with your partners' market presence and customer relationships.

**Benefits for Your Company:**
- Extended market reach through partner channels
- Shared marketing investment and reduced cost per lead
- Local market expertise and presence
- Increased brand awareness in partner ecosystems

**Benefits for Partners:**
- Access to your marketing resources and expertise
- Co-branded content and professional assets
- Financial support through MDF programs
- Demand generation for their pipeline

### Program Principles
- **Brand Integrity:** All co-marketing must adhere to brand guidelines
- **Mutual Benefit:** Activities should generate value for both parties
- **Measurable Impact:** Track and report on all co-marketing ROI
- **Scalable Process:** Standardized workflows that work for all partner tiers

## 3. Brand Guidelines for Partners

### Logo Usage
```
DO:
- Use approved co-branded logo lockups
- Maintain minimum clear space around logos
- Use logos on approved background colors
- Scale logos proportionally

DON'T:
- Alter logo colors or proportions
- Place logos on busy backgrounds
- Use outdated logo versions
- Create unauthorized logo combinations
```

### Co-Branding Standards
| Element | Guideline |
|---------|-----------|
| Logo Placement | Your logo left, partner logo right (or as specified) |
| Logo Size | Equal visual weight for both logos |
| Color Usage | Primary brand colors only, no alterations |
| Typography | Approved fonts only (provide font files) |
| Imagery | Use approved image library or partner-provided |

### Messaging Guidelines
- Always use approved product names and descriptions
- Include required legal disclaimers and trademarks
- Avoid comparative claims without approval
- Submit all external-facing content for review

### Asset Library Access
Provide partners access to:
- [ ] Logo files (various formats: PNG, SVG, EPS)
- [ ] Brand guidelines document
- [ ] Approved photography and illustrations
- [ ] PowerPoint/Google Slides templates
- [ ] Email templates
- [ ] Social media graphics and copy
- [ ] Product screenshots and demo videos

## 4. Market Development Funds (MDF)

### MDF Program Structure
| Partner Tier | Annual MDF Allocation | Funding Model |
|--------------|----------------------|---------------|
| Registered | Not eligible | - |
| Silver | Up to $5,000 | 50% co-investment |
| Gold | Up to $15,000 | 50% co-investment |
| Platinum | Up to $50,000+ | 50-75% co-investment |

### Eligible Activities
**Approved for MDF:**
- Joint webinars and virtual events
- Local events and trade shows
- Digital advertising (PPC, social, display)
- Content development (case studies, whitepapers)
- Direct mail campaigns
- Partner website development (solution pages)

**Not Eligible for MDF:**
- General partner overhead or salaries
- Non-marketing travel expenses
- Activities not featuring your joint value proposition
- Alcohol or entertainment expenses
- Activities without lead capture mechanism

### MDF Request Process
```
Step 1: Submit Request (30+ days before activity)
        Partner completes MDF request form with:
        - Activity description and objectives
        - Target audience and expected reach
        - Detailed budget breakdown
        - Lead capture and follow-up plan
        - Metrics for success measurement

Step 2: Review & Approval (5-7 business days)
        Partner marketing team reviews for:
        - Strategic alignment
        - Budget appropriateness
        - Brand compliance
        - Expected ROI

Step 3: Execution
        Partner executes activity per approved plan
        Your team provides agreed support

Step 4: Proof of Performance (30 days post-activity)
        Partner submits:
        - Receipts and invoices
        - Screenshots/photos of activity
        - Lead list with required fields
        - Results summary

Step 5: Reimbursement (30 days after POP approval)
        Finance processes reimbursement to partner
```

### MDF Request Form Fields
```
Partner Information:
- Partner company name
- Contact name and email
- Partner tier

Activity Details:
- Activity type (from approved list)
- Activity name/description
- Target audience and geography
- Proposed date(s)
- Expected attendees/reach

Budget:
- Total activity cost
- MDF amount requested
- Partner co-investment amount
- Line-item budget breakdown

Goals & Measurement:
- Number of leads expected
- Other KPIs (registrations, attendees, etc.)
- Follow-up plan for leads generated

Attachments:
- Draft creative/messaging (for review)
- Vendor quotes (if applicable)
```

## 6. Lead Management & Attribution

### Lead Capture Requirements
All co-marketing activities must capture:
- First name, last name
- Company name
- Job title
- Business email
- Phone number (optional but preferred)
- Consent for marketing communications

### Lead Routing
```
Lead Source          → Routing
-----------------------------------------
Your-hosted webinar  → Your CRM, share with partner
Partner-hosted event → Partner receives first, copy to your CRM
Joint campaign       → Split based on agreed rules
```

### Attribution Model
| Scenario | Attribution |
|----------|-------------|
| Partner sources lead, you close | Partner-sourced |
| Joint activity generates lead | Partner-influenced |
| Your campaign, partner assists close | Partner-assisted |

### SLA for Lead Follow-up
- Hot leads: Contact within 24 hours
- Warm leads: Contact within 48 hours
- Nurture leads: Add to appropriate nurture track

## 8. Measuring Co-Marketing Success

### KPIs to Track
| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Leads Generated | Total new contacts from activity | Activity-specific |
| Cost Per Lead | Total spend / leads generated | < $100 |
| Pipeline Created | $ value of opportunities | 5x MDF investment |
| Win Rate | % of leads that close | Benchmark to direct |
| Partner Engagement | % of partners using co-marketing | 40%+ of Gold/Platinum |

### Reporting Cadence
- **Monthly:** Activity summary, leads generated, MDF utilization
- **Quarterly:** ROI analysis, top-performing partners, program improvements
- **Annually:** Full program review, budget planning, strategy refinement

### ROI Calculation
```
Co-Marketing ROI = (Pipeline Created - Total Investment) / Total Investment x 100

Example:
- MDF Spent: $10,000
- Your Additional Investment: $5,000
- Pipeline Created: $150,000
- ROI = ($150,000 - $15,000) / $15,000 x 100 = 900%
```
